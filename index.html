Political Science · Public Policy · Internet Governance

I work on political analysis and public policy, with a growing interest in international governance.

I am a Political Science graduate based in Amsterdam, currently working in the Public Policy and Internet Governance department at RIPE NCC. My work and research focus on how domestic division, political structures and party politics shape decision making domestically and internationally, with a particular focus on Latin America. Using a mixed method approach, I study how demographic differences between individuals and communities at a local level get translated at a national level, and how this affects partys positioning on foreign policy issues.

I am especially interested in the point where domestic politics stops being domestic.

What this site is for

This website brings together my academic work, research interests, and professional profile. It is meant as a space to present current projects, selected writing, and the questions that shape my approach to politics and policy.

I am particularly drawn to political analysis that links structure and agency: how broad social divisions shape party systems, and how those same divisions reappear in decisions that are often presented as purely strategic or external.

Selected focus

Thesis

Foreign policy and cleavage theory

My thesis examines whether party positions on foreign policy in Mexico and Colombia reflect domestic social cleavages rather than purely leader-driven choices.
